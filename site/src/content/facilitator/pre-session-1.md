*If sending by email, attach the slide as an image or paste it inline. It should be genuinely catastrophic — 11+ bullet points, tiny font, clip art, WordArt, garish colours, a graph with no labels. Title it something recognisably clinical, such as "Patient Safety Update — All Staff." See the Session 1 blueprint if you have not made it yet.*

## Pulse survey

Send alongside or before the email, or embed as a link. Use Google Forms, Microsoft Forms, or any equivalent. Maximum 3 questions. Responses inform how you open Session 1.

> **1.** What is your biggest teaching challenge right now?
> *(free text)*
>
> **2.** When did you last receive any feedback on your teaching?
> *(free text, or options: in the last month / in the last 6 months / over a year ago / never)*
>
> **3.** What do your learners need from you that they are currently not getting?
> *(free text)*

### Using the responses

Read all responses before the session. Look for:

- Common themes in challenge 1 — these tell you what is live for this cohort
- Responses to question 2 — if the majority have not had feedback in over a year, name this in the session (psychological safety context)
- Responses to question 3 — these are your constructivist foundations; the needs your participants identify are the content you build from

You do not need to share the responses verbatim in the session. Use them to calibrate your facilitation — which examples to emphasise, where resistance is likely, what needs to be named early.
