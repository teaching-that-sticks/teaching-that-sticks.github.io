## How to use this template

A task and finish group exists to scope, initiate, and hand off a defined piece of work. Running a cohort of Teaching That Sticks is that piece of work.

The tables below prompt the questions good project initiation requires. You do not need a project management background to complete them — you need an hour, a colleague to sense-check, and the discipline to commit to answers before you start booking rooms and sending emails.

**Rule:** if a row is blank on the day you send the first participant invite, you have not finished planning.

---

## 1. Programme identity

| Question | Your answer |
|----------|-------------|
| **What is the explicit aim?** | What specific change in educator behaviour are you trying to produce? Not "better teaching" — what specifically better? |
| **How will you know it worked?** | Name the Level 3 measure. Who will ask participants what changed, and when? |
| **What counts as success?** | Define it before delivery. If 80% of participants name one change they made — is that success? Set the bar now. |
| **What is the scope?** | Which staff groups? Which sites? How many cohorts? Pilot first, or full roll-out? |
| **Who does this programme belong to?** | Which directorate, department, or individual is accountable for delivery and evaluation? |

---

## 2. Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Their interest | What they need from you | When |
|-------------|---------------|------------------------|------|
| **Executive sponsor** | Strategic alignment; risk awareness | One-page brief (see *The Case for This Programme*); clear ask (protected time, sign-off) | Before announcement |
| **Line managers of participants** | Release time; expectation of what changes | Short email: what this is, what you are asking, what to expect differently afterwards | Before invites go out |
| **Participants themselves** | Why this applies to them; what it will ask of them | Honest framing — this is not a passive study day; you will be asked to change things | Invite email |
| **Facilitator** | Brief, support, debrief after each session | Full blueprint access; named supervisor or peer to debrief with | Before Session 1 |
| **HR / L&D / CPD lead** | Portfolio alignment; revalidation mapping | Session attendance record; Kirkpatrick Level 1 data; framework alignment if required | After programme |
| **Anyone else?** | | | |

> **Note:** The most commonly missed stakeholder is the line manager of participants. If managers do not know what changed and why, they cannot reinforce it. The New World Kirkpatrick Model identifies managerial reinforcement as a Required Driver for Level 3 behaviour change. Brief them before the programme, not after.

---

## 3. Participants

| Question | Your answer |
|----------|-------------|
| **Who is the target group?** | Role, grade, band, specialty, number |
| **How will they be selected?** | Nominated, self-selected, mandatory? (Note: mandatory attendance is not neutral — it affects psychological safety in Session 2. Flag it if relevant.) |
| **What do they already know?** | Any prior educator training? Will they arrive as a cohort (know each other) or strangers? |
| **What are the equity considerations?** | Dyslexia, English as additional language, shift patterns preventing consistent attendance, childcare constraints — identify and plan for these *before* invitations |
| **What is the minimum viable cohort?** | Below 8, group activities lose energy. If you cannot confirm 8 by two weeks before Session 1, postpone rather than deliver to a half-empty room. |

## 6. Evaluation design

Design this before Session 1, not after Session 3.

| Level | What you will measure | Method | Owner | When |
|-------|----------------------|--------|-------|------|
| **L1 — Reaction** | Did participants find it valuable? | Padlet prompt (end of each session) | Facilitator | End of each session |
| **L2 — Learning** | Can they apply the principles? | Session 3 retrieval quiz | Facilitator | Session 3 opening |
| **L3 — Behaviour** | What changed in their teaching practice? | Three-question email survey | Programme lead | 30 / 60 / 90 days post-programme |
| **L4 — Results** | Did their learners learn more? | Local education metrics, incident data, exam results — optional | Clinical lead | 6 months+ |

> **Critical point:** Without a pre-planned L3 follow-up mechanism, this programme will stop at Level 2. The 30-day follow-up is not optional. Name the owner and the date now, while motivation is high.

---

## 7. Risks and mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Participants pulled to clinical duties mid-programme | High | High | Line manager briefed in advance; protected time confirmed in writing before invitations sent |
| Facilitator unavailable on session day | Medium | High | Named cover facilitator briefed and holding all materials |
| Low attendance at Session 2 or 3 | Medium | High | Pre-session reminder 48 hours before; Padlet commitment from Session 1 acts as accountability mechanism |
| Participants attend mandatorily and arrive disengaged | Medium | Medium | Facilitator brief includes guidance on opening with honest framing: *"I know some of you didn't choose this. Here's why it might matter anyway."* |
| Tech failure | High | Low | Analogue fallbacks in every blueprint; tech failure explicitly modelled as a teaching moment |
| No Level 3 data collected | High | High | **Appoint the follow-up owner in this document, today.** |
| Management does not reinforce changed practice | High | High | Line manager briefing (see Stakeholders); include managers in 90-day comms |

> **Add your own rows.** The risks you can predict are the ones you can mitigate. The ones you skip here are the ones that will surprise you.

## Sign-off

Before you begin delivery, confirm that the following are in place:

- [ ] Sponsor or commissioning lead has reviewed *The Case for This Programme* and confirmed support
- [ ] All three session dates are booked and confirmed with the venue
- [ ] All participants have been invited and confirmed
- [ ] Line managers of participants have been briefed
- [ ] Facilitator is named, briefed, and has read all three blueprints
- [ ] Cover facilitator is named and briefed
- [ ] Level 3 evaluation owner is named and dates are in their diary
- [ ] Padlet is created and tested

**If any row above is blank, you are not ready to send Session 1 invitations.**
